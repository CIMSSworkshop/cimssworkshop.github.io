Securing the Future: Advanced Safety and Resilience in Autonomous and Autonomic Systems

Shreekant Thakkar

Abstract: Dr. Shreekant Thakkar's presentation emphasizes the critical role of autonomous and autonomic systems in managing complex, scalable operations like smart cities, while highlighting the increasing security vulnerabilities. He proposes a comprehensive approach to ensure end-to-end security, resilience, and safety in these systems, leveraging Zero Trust architecture and Secure Mesh Shield. The presentation also outlines the operation of Zero Trust Autonomous System Platforms and introduces a framework for autonomic system platform safety, focusing on secure networking, scalability, collective intelligence, and ethical alignment.

Bio: Dr Shreekant (Ticky) Thakkar is Chief Research Officer at the Secure Systems Research Centre at the Technology Innovation Institute (TII), a cutting-edge UAE-based scientific research Centre and Adjunct Research Professor at Khalifa University. In this role, he is responsible for carrying out advanced research that is driving end-to-end security and resilience in cyber physical and autonomous systems of systems (swarm of drones). These includes secure technologies in silicon, edge and mobile and cloud platforms working with open-source ecosystems (Dronecode, RISC-V, Linux, Apache, ROS) and research institutions across USA, Europe, and UAE.

Security of Advanced Machine Learning Features in Autonomous Systems

Muhammad Shafique

Abstract: Modern Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) approaches, such as, the Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), have shown tremendous improvement over the past years to achieve a significantly high accuracy for a certain set of tasks, like image classification, object detection, natural language processing, and medical data analytics. These DNNs are deployed in s a wide range of autonomous systems, from the domains of Smart Cyber Physical Systems (CPS) and Internet of Thing (IoT), on resource-constrained devices subjected to unpredictable and harsh scenarios, thereby requiring secure and robust AI solutions. Moreover, in the era of growing cyber-security threats, the intelligent features of autonomous systems face new type of attacks, requiring novel design principles for robust ML/AI.

In my eBRAIN lab at New York University (AD, US), I have been extensively investigating the foundations for the next-generation energy-efficient, dependable, and secure AI/ML computing systems, while addressing the above-mentioned challenges across the hardware and software stacks. This talk will present design challenges and advanced techniques for building secure and robust AI systems, which leverage optimizations at different layers of the system stack, and at different design stages (e.g., design-time vs. run-time approaches). These techniques provide crucial steps towards enabling the wide-scale deployment of secure embedded AI in autonomous systems like UAVs, UGVs, autonomous vehicles, Robotics, Industrial-IoT, smart transportation, etc.
